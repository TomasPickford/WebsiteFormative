Positive Impacts of Technology in Hopsitals

100 years ago, the medical history of a patient would written on paper kept in a folder, prone to damage and expensive to store. Getting medical history from a doctor today is as easy as searching for your name on a computer where millions of records can be kept and backed up efficiently. Technology has impacted our society in many ways from expansion of communication to even banking online. However, the positive impacts of technology in hospitals cannot be undermined I will discuss how exactly technology has revolutionised certain processes in healthcare. Recording data, analysing patients, finding new answers using simulations and even using drones to transport goods to remote areas are all examples of improvements made to the smooth running and efficiency of nowadays hospitals.

Electronical medical records (EHR) consist of digital summaries of patient medical records possibly including diagnoses, lab reports, prescriptions and more. The electronic records facilitate the easy sharing of information and collaboration between labs and specialists without spending a lot on physically transmission of information. EHR records are easier and less time consuming to create, maintain and store. EHR is a standardised protocol which is used globally, this reduces the chances of mistakes being made since the data has to be entered a certain way to work. Further advantages of using EHR is that it prevents the patients from seeing multiple specialists, smooth transitions between care settings and offering evidence-based information. Imagine if we still used paper records, if you moved the files would need
to be transported through the traditional mailing system, which could be lost through transmission. If your situation required immediate attention, then this could be fatal.
