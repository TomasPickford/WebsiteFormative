AI Mental Health Guidance

It’s no secret that in the modern day and age, the topic of mental health is indeed a very contemporary topic and is ever present in the online conversation. With one source showing that 1 in 4 people experience mental health issues each year, it really does make us wonder why we aren’t doing much more to aid this significant portion of the population (which at some point is likely to include ourselves) in order to achieve the happiness in life which Americas have the legal right to pursue as should the rest of the world.

But wait, how are we going to use 0s and 1s to solve real, human problems? Surely a simple computer program is too rigid to solve problems as undeniably complex as those to do with mental health where there are thousands of factors contributing to someone’s mental state and yet even more factors affecting how someone may react to a certain situation. Not only that, but do we really trust computer programs to be able to make the right suggestion where often times there isn’t one? Not to mention that a bug (which are inevitable in all programs) could lead to a person feeling left abandoned and alone or being given the wrong suggestion and leading to a subsequent rash decision which could be life-threatening.
Despite all of this, it’s not all doom and gloom! A company Crisis Text Linehas figured out a way to use artificial intelligence with their 24/7 text service to enhance the advice which users are given. Instead of simply having the user interface directly with an AI chatbot, they have used AI as an advice tool for the counsellors themselves. If a counsellor is unsure of what to do in a given situation, they can consult an AI which can offer some suggestions as to which path to take in helping the individual. This is especially useful given that often the counsellor are just kind and caring volunteers looking to make a positive impact on the world with no formal previous experience. Through their training with Crisis Text Line and the help of AI they can greatly increase their chance of being able to help those people for whom they signed up.

The AI was developed through the analysis of over 1 million anonymous texts from nearly 3,500 counsellors. Using this data, the researchers were able to determine trends in the way that counsellor aided users and how they changed their tone, and approach depending on the situation and severity of the situation. This kind of task is notoriously difficult because human language is very difficult for computers to truly comprehend and be able to replicate. Therefore, to achieve this task, the researchers used a technique known as NLP, Natural Language Processing. One finding from this research was that inexperienced counsellors stuck to formal language, where as experienced counsellors made more personable and informal vocabulary choices

Where will this go in the future? Well, the infamous Turing test (where a human being is unable to discern whether they are talking to a human or a computer over text) has already been passed, which allows us to entertain the possibility that in the future we may even be able to have fully automated counselling services. However, reason seems to stand that if the user knows they are simply talking to a computer program which has no feelings nor empathy for them whatsoever, even if the exact same words are spoken, the psychological outcome will be different. It is theorised that eventually we will be able to develop a form of AI known as Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) which is AI that can comprehend any task and information in the same way that we humans can, it seems more probable that once we have developed such an AI, automated counselling may be more feasible.

Walter White
